Bike Trails Needed to Complete in Texas

The Lone Star State is one of the best places to ride your bike. It is full of a variety of terrain, whether road or mountain. Capture yourself with air blowing your hair and the sun, making your ride magical.

Willow City Loop

  • Distance: 55.8 miles
  • Elevation: 2,877 feet
  • Type: loop

One of Texas's most loved bike routes. This ride will show you nature in the heart of the Hill Country, with its rural landscape and, at the right time - the abundance of wildflowers.

Walnut Creek Metropolitan Park

  • Distance: 10.5 miles
  • Elevation: 697 feet
  • Type: loop

Walnut Creek Metro Park has many trails for BMX and mountain biking. The terrain is full of jumps and gorgeous views in the heart of Austin, TX.

Northeast Texas Trail

  • Distance: 133 miles
  • Elevation: 1,944 feet
  • Type: one-way

Northeast Texas Trail is not for the faint of heart. It spans from the Dallas-Fort Worth area to Texarkana and follows the Union Pacific and Chaparral railroads. You will need a mountain bike, as the roads are not paved.

River Legacy Park Trail

  • Distance: 10 miles
  • Elevation: 301 feet
  • Type: loop

River Legacy Bike Trail is perfect for road or mountain biking. It is located in Arlington, TX, and accommodates beginners to advanced riders.

Big Bend Ranch State Park

  • Distance: 239 miles
  • Elevation: 12,000 feet
  • Type: one-way

Big Bend Ranch State Park trails rank at the top of the country. A vast desert playground full of the past ranchers, Native Americans, and Spaniards. The stunning natural single-track trails are a winding web of fantastic scenery.

Palo Duro Canyon

  • Distance: 66 miles
  • Elevation: 3,500 feet
  • Type: one-way

Palo Duro Canyon is located in the Texas Panhandle and has trails for all levels of adventurous bikers. Bikers will wind their way through the second-largest canyon in the US.
